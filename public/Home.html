Pyramids

The history of pyramids dates back to around 2500 BCE in Mesopotamia, where the first known pyramid-like structure, the ziggurat, was built. The Egyptians, who are famous for their pyramids, started constructing these monumental structures around 2600 BCE as tombs for pharaohs. The Great Pyramid of Giza, the oldest and largest of the three pyramids on the Giza plateau, was built around 2560 BCE and is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Dimensions of History

History is often characterized by the use of dimensions to organize and interpret events. For example, historians often use temporal, spatial, and intellectual dimensions to understand the past. They may study the chronological unfolding of events, the geographical locations in which they occur, and the cultural, social, and philosophical contexts in which they are situated.
